01 - The Problem

The product was good. The drop-off wasn't about the product.

This company had a strong product, a capable CS team, and a real retention problem. Customers were churning or escalating in the first 30 days - not because the software didn't work, but because they didn't understand how to make it work for their specific situation.

The CS team was compensating with manual check-in calls. A rep would get on a 30-minute call to walk a customer through something that could have been a 5-minute read. Multiply that by hundreds of customers per month and you have a margin problem dressed up as a support problem.

"We knew the education gap existed. We just didn't have the bandwidth to close it ourselves - every hour the team spent writing was an hour they weren't spending with customers."

Three friction points kept surfacing in CS call logs. Customers consistently got stuck in the same places: initial configuration, integrating with their existing stack, and understanding which features applied to their use case. These weren't product flaws - they were content gaps.

02 - The Approach

Map the friction. Brief against it. Build fast.

The brief wasn't "write onboarding articles." The brief was: find the three moments where customers lose confidence, understand exactly what they're confused about, and produce content that removes that confusion before a CS rep ever has to make a call.

1

Audit the friction points

Pulled 3 months of CS call notes, Intercom transcripts, and support ticket themes. Three distinct failure moments emerged, each with a clear knowledge gap underneath it.

2

Build brand-specific briefs

Each article got a brief specifying: the customer segment, the exact question it needed to answer, tone and voice parameters, and the action it needed to drive. Every brief was tied to a real failure moment.

3

AI-assisted drafting with recursive editorial checks

AI handled the structural draft. The editorial layer handled brand voice, accuracy, and judgment calls a model can't make. No article left the pipeline without passing a checklist tied to the original brief.

4

Deploy and measure against CS data

Articles were embedded in the onboarding flow at the exact moments of documented friction. CS call logs were monitored for 6 weeks post-launch.

03 - The Results

The content did the job the calls were doing.

Six weeks after deployment, the three friction points had measurably declined in CS call volume. Customers were self-resolving more, escalating less, and completing onboarding milestones at a higher rate.

04 - What Made It Work

The brief was the product. The writing was the execution.

Most AI-assisted content programs fail at the brief, not the draft. A model given a generic topic and a word count will produce generic output. What changed the outcome here was starting with a specific question a specific customer was asking at a specific moment in their journey, and building the entire brief around answering that question cleanly, in the brand's voice, at the right depth.

The AI layer allowed the work to move fast. The editorial layer ensured it actually worked. Neither alone would have produced the result.
